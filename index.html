About Me

I am Dr. Tufan Roy, a computational materials scientist specializing in spintronics and magnetic materials. As an Assistant Professor at Tohoku University’s Center for Science and Innovation in Spintronics, I am currently investigating magneto-transport phenomena in kagome magnets and Heusler alloys through an integrated methodology that combines first-principles calculations with data-driven analysis.

Education

    Thesis: Effect of Substitution on Electronic Structure, Magnetic and Mechanical Properties of Ni-, Pt-, and Mn-based Heusler Alloys
  • Master of Science (M.Sc), The University of Burdwan (2012)
  • Bachelor of Science (B.Sc (H)), The University of Burdwan (2010)

Research Experience

  • Assistant Professor, Tohoku University, Japan | Nov. 2022 – Present
  • Specially Appointed Assistant Professor, Tohoku University | Jul. 2021 – Oct. 2022
  • Postdoctoral Researcher, Tohoku University, Japan | May 2018 – Jun. 2021
    Prime research interest: Computational design of magnetic tunnel junctions with high TMR at room temperature, using Heusler alloys and MgO barriers.
  • Postdoctoral Research Associate, RRCAT, Indore, India | Oct. 2017 – Mar. 2018
    Continued research on magnetic shape memory Heusler alloys following Ph.D. submission.
